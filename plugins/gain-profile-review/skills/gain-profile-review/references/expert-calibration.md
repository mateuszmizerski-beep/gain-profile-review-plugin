# Expert Calibration

Read this file before every Gain profile review. These rules reflect direct expert-reviewer corrections and override conflicting live-profile precedent.

## Editing posture

- Preserve acceptable analyst wording. Change only what is inaccurate, inconsistent, incomplete, unsupported, unclear, or materially weaker than an available formulation.
- Do not rewrite for novelty or to demonstrate activity.
- Distinguish mandatory corrections from optional polish.
- Existing live profiles establish tone and structure but can contain errors.

## Business sentence rhythm

- Treat a profile containing subsidiaries and/or brands as a Group profile.
- In a Group profile, start with the asset name, alternate with `the Group` where practical, and never use `the Company` in any of the three Business blocks.
- In a standalone-company profile, start with the asset name, alternate with `the Company` where practical, and do not use `the Group`.
- `Company/Group` means choose the correct term from the profile perimeter; it does not mean the two terms are interchangeable.
- Do not replace the subject with `it` or `its` merely for variety.
- Conventional Gain wording is acceptable when clear.
- Nestlé is a practical Group example: its profile alternates `Nestlé` and `the Group` throughout At a glance, Customer base and Competitive position without using `the Company`.

## Tags

- The platform has a limited predefined tag taxonomy, so a conceptually good tag may not be available.
- Treat tag additions and cleanup as reviewer-discretion unless the selected subsector is missing, an existing tag is factually wrong, or the tag set materially misrepresents the business.
- Recommend an exact tag only when its platform availability is known. Otherwise suggest checking the nearest available tag or describe the missing concept without presenting it as a required fix.

## Aliases and punctuation severity

- Treat missing aliases, including subsidiary, brand, trading-name and normalized-character aliases, as reviewer-discretion by default.
- An alias becomes a critical fix only when an existing alias is factually wrong, points to another business, or the alias set creates a material identity/perimeter contradiction.
- Oxford-comma cleanup is reviewer-discretion and does not block publication.
- Escalate punctuation only when it changes or materially obscures meaning.

## Review response structure

- Organize review feedback by: Key Facts; Financials; Business, split into Business model text, Customer base text and Competitive position text; Ownership; Assessment; and Cross-section consistency when needed.
- Within every section, distinguish `Critical fix` from `Reviewer discretion`. Critical fixes affect factual accuracy, mandatory completeness, financial logic, ownership, or cross-section consistency; discretionary points are interpretive, stylistic, or optional enhancements.
- Use `Publishable` when no critical fixes remain, even if discretionary suggestions remain. Use `Publishable after changes` when critical fixes remain but are reviewer-fixable. Reserve `Return to analyst` for exceptional extensive or unresolvable work.
- Put exact replacement wording under the relevant section finding rather than in a detached rewrite section.
- Mark a section as `Pass` when it has been checked and no issue arises.

## Segmentations and hidden Excel fields

- Financial segmentations reconcile to `Net revenue` in the Gain Excel workbook, not necessarily Total revenue exposed through MCP.
- If Net revenue is unavailable, disclose the limitation. Do not call a segmentation wrong by comparing it only with Total revenue.
- Review cash conversion using Excel's `Cash conversion - Capex-based` metric.
- Do not challenge cash conversion using a simplified `(EBITDA - CAPEX) / EBITDA` calculation when the Excel metric is unavailable.

## Sales channels

- A company selling directly to a distributor, wholesaler, garden centre, retailer, importer, exporter, trader, or dealer normally uses Direct sales because that counterparty buys from the company.
- Do not select Indirect sales merely because the direct customer later resells the product.
- Indirect sales requires a transaction intermediary that does not purchase the product as the company's customer, e.g. Steam facilitating a publisher's game sale.
- Online and Brick-and-mortar remain subject to the approximately 5% materiality threshold.

## Targeted gap research

- Treat review as an evidence check, not only a comparison of fields already populated in Gain. Key Facts and prose can repeat the same omission.
- Research independently when a consequential unknown could change customer-base ticks, revenue-model wording, ownership, market position, a material P&C, or an Assessment rating.
- For healthcare and similar funded sectors, trace the payment path separately across self-pay customers, private insurers or corporate payers, and public reimbursement bodies. The patient or end-user is not necessarily the only customer.
- Distinguish existence, materiality, and predictability. Evidence that public reimbursement or an insurer contract exists can support B2G or B2B, but it does not establish a material revenue share without further evidence.
- Do not raise revenue visibility merely because sales are described as contractual or reimbursed. Determine whether contracts provide committed volumes or revenue, or merely eligibility, tariffs, and settlement terms. Also consider episodic demand, recurrence, backlog, switching costs, and contract duration.
- Monza Ares is the calibration example: official pages showed partial Romanian Health Insurance House reimbursement and substantial patient co-payments, supporting B2G and B2C routes. Private-insurer contracts support a possible B2B route. The payer mix remained unquantified, and reimbursement did not by itself justify increasing revenue visibility from three to four stars.
- Keep research bounded. Check the most likely primary sources first and stop when further searching is unlikely to alter the review. Escalate a concise residual question to the expert only when the unresolved judgment is material.

## Finding-level source links

- Every finding that introduces or relies on external research must include a clickable source link directly beneath the finding. This is a total requirement for future review runs.
- An end-of-review source list is not sufficient. Repeat the relevant source below each externally researched factual correction, calculation, inference, rating challenge, ownership point, portfolio change or rewrite.
- These links are review evidence only. Do not insert parenthetical website citations into proposed Gain Business prose except under the separate employee-estimate rule.
- Label calculations and inferences transparently. Show the sourced inputs for a materiality calculation and state when a conclusion is reviewer judgment rather than wording used by the source.
- Do not overstate what a source proves. Evidence of some framework agreements, recurring services or contractual relationships does not establish the group-wide contractual revenue share unless that share is disclosed or defensibly calculable.
- Tången calibration: its prospectus states that public-sector customers represent approximately 30% of the customer base. Rental income was SEK 2.1m of SEK 1,595.2m 2025 net sales, approximately 0.13%; calling it immaterial is a sourced calculation, not a directly quoted company conclusion. The prospectus supports recurring service relationships and some public framework agreements but does not establish that the profile's group-wide contractual revenue description is overstated.
- The Tången founder correction concerned spacing, not founder identity. `Kasper Geforsand` and `Fredrik Löfgrenand` are concatenation/word-boundary errors for `Kasper Gefors and Fredrik Löfgren`; the three individuals remain the founders.

## Last deal date

- The asset being PE/VC-backed is a necessary gate, but it is not sufficient for every later transaction to update Last deal date.
- Use only a deal in which the PE/VC investor itself enters, exits, provides ownership funding, or changes its stake in the profiled asset.
- Do not update Last deal date for an add-on acquisition, operating-company merger, or issuance of minority shares to founders, doctors, managers, or other non-PE/VC holders when the PE/VC investor's ownership is unchanged.
- Monza Ares is the calibration example: the May 2026 Brain Institute merger brought physician shareholders into the Group, but Highlander Partners remained the existing majority owner without a disclosed PE stake transaction. Therefore, the July 2019 Highlander investment remains the Last deal date.

## Pros and Cons

- Review the analyst's submitted P&Cs point by point before suggesting newly drafted alternatives.
- Label every submitted argument by position (`P1`-`P3`, `C1`-`C3`) and assign one verdict: `Keep`, `Improve`, `Replace`, or `Remove`.
- Explain both strengths and defects. Consider factual accuracy, investor relevance, causal logic, company specificity, ranking, metric identity, timeframe and character discipline.
- Preserve the original idea whenever it can be made client-ready through a limited edit. A new argument is not automatically better than an acceptable analyst point.
- Only introduce a new point for a blank slot, an unusable original, or a clearly stronger replacement for a weak lower-ranked argument. Identify exactly which point it replaces.
- When no existing Pros or Cons are populated, explicitly say so before drafting candidate points. Galeco is the calibration example: Gain exposed a blank Assessment, so the review should have stated `No existing Pros/Cons to audit` rather than appearing to ignore analyst-written points.
- Preserve standard parenthetical evidence: `Leading position ... (+8% CAGR 2019-2024)`.
- Never remove a statistic's year or period while shortening an argument.
- Do not claim a production-footprint KPI supports EBITDA margin unless evidence or clear operating logic establishes the connection.
- A useful KPI may be inserted without tying it to an unrelated financial outcome.
- Prefer the original argument when it already matches typical Gain wording and is analytically sound.
- Quantify when useful, especially downside, but do not force a number.

## Market wording

- Use `claims to be a leader` when the company itself makes the leadership claim.
- Avoid marketing formulations such as `award-winning product design` unless the award is itself material and described factually.
- Strong recent share or ranking evidence may stand without a separate differentiation point.
- Current market-share, ranking, and market-size evidence should generally be no more than four years old.

## Completeness and judgment

- Reviewers normally research and write missing useful content rather than returning the profile.
- Market may remain blank only when there is genuinely nothing defensible to say.
- Three Pros and three Cons remain the default, but either reviewer may approve two strong points rather than a weak third.
- Employee/FTE information may be omitted after reasonable research when unavailable.
- Ownership verification has no separate freshness threshold; use the latest reliable information.
- For PE/VC ownership, name the investors. For a private individual's holding company, include the holding company and/or individual when useful.

## Ownership continuity wording

- Use `remained` when ownership by the named person, family or investor has continued unchanged since founding or the preceding ownership event.
- Use `was` when giving only a dated ownership snapshot, or when uninterrupted continuity has not been established.
- `As of June 2026, the Company remained solely owned by the Żuk family` is appropriate because KOME Polska has remained under the founding family's ownership.
- The prohibited vague wording is `remained privately owned` without naming the owners. Do not generalize that warning into replacing every precise use of `remained` with `was`.

## Company-website sourcing

- Do not cite the company or group website parenthetically for ordinary operating KPIs such as facilities, production space, capacity, locations, customers, or brands.
- A company/group website may still be used to research and verify those facts without being named in the Business sentence.
- The exception is a newer employee estimate published on the website. Attribute it in the employee sentence, e.g. `had 100 employees as of June 2026 (Company website)` or `(Group website)` as applicable.
